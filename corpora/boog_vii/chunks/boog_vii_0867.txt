An unprecedented organizational structure for the efficient deployment of the combined human and material resources of two independent states, the CCS was an important factor in making Allied conduct of the war superior to that of the Axis powers. As well as establishing a joint command structure, the Allies managed to agree in Washington on the outlines of their future military strategy against Germany.
