In particular, he demanded that all depots be decentralized and many supply facilities be moved to smaller towns or villages away from main roads and railway stations. All trucks and supply points were to be shielded against bomb splinters, and lines of communication doubled. It must have come as a great surprise to the senior quartermaster when he discovered, on an inspection tour of the Fifteenth Army, that at the end off January 1944 its supply system was still organized as if the whole army were about to be moved to England.
