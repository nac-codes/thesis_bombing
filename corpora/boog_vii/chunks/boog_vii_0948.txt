Smaller air units were stationed on the southern Atlantic coast and in the Rhône delta. In the second half of 1943 the bomber units deployed in the west (especially IX Air Corps) mainly attacked London residential districts. In early 1944, when the Allied build-up became clearer, they switched their attacks to concentrations of ships and port installations (Bristol, Hull, Portsmouth, and elsewhere), though with no great success. The Allied air defences not only beat them off, but inflicted ever greater losses on the German units.
