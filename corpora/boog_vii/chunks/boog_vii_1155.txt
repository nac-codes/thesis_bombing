In fact, the morale of the troops in the south of France seems to have been highly ambivalent. Whether or not soldiers and officers would stand up to a massive Allied assault depended on several factors. A division with a long tradition behind it, covered in glory, would certainly fight more bravely than formations that had been patched together from all sorts of different units. There was less fear of desertion or of positions being abandoned pre- maturely in areas where the men's superiors, with the assistance of Nazi propaganda officers, had worked intensively on the soldiers.
