At the same time, US 7th Army was driving the Germans back eastwards across the Vosges near St-Die. The Allies now also succeeded in breaking through the German positions in the Belfort Gap north of the Swiss border. On 20 November they reached the Rhine and two days later seized Mulhouse. The Nineteenth Army did, how- ever, manage to hold a large bridgehead at Colmar. The French Ist Army proved too weak to advance northwards along the Rhine at this stage.27 The North African soldiers in De Lattre de Tassigny's units found the atrocious weather particularly dispiriting; their morale also began to suffer because many of them had the impression they were being made to run all the risks on behalf of comrades from the mother country who seemed rather reluctant to join the French army.
