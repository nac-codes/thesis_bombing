Churchill had already been leaning in the Americans’ direction. Air Marshal Sir Charles Portal, raf Chief of the Air Staff, wrote the prime min- ister in early November 1942 endorsing the American daylight effort, and in mid-December Air Marshal John Slessor and Secretary of State for Air Sir Archibald Sinclair added their endorsements. In part, the British believed that daylight bombing might work; in part, the British did not want to risk that the American bomber force might transfer to the Pacific theater.
