Never before or since has a military machine of such size and technical com- plexity been created in so short a period, At the height of World War I, Arnold commanded the largest, mightiest air force the world had ever seen and perhaps will ever see. When his B-29s dropped the atomic bombs on Japan in 1945, the U.S. Air Force dominated the entire world  In the process of building this gigantic and unprecedented air fleet, Arnold had spent several billions of American taxpayers’ dollars. But when he died in 1950, his entire personal estate amounted to only two $10,000 insurance policies and his “ranch,” for which he had paid  $7,500.
