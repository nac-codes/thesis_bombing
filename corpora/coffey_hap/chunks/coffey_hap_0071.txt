On the day of the examination, he was there with pen in hand, and  to the amazement of everyone concerned, including himself and his  high school principal, who had scoffed at the notion that Henry might be admitted to the Military Academy, he came in second, He had done remarkably well for a high-spirited youngster who had always seemed allergic to books, well enough, in fact, to frighten him with the hitherto unthinkable notion that he might actually have to go to the academy. But he was soon relieved of that fear when he learned that only one appointment was to be granted and it would go to the man who had come in first  Quite contentedly, Henry Arnold resumed his plans for Bucknell and the Baptist ministry, but once again his fate took an unexpected turn, The young man who had won the appointment announced, the evening he was to leave for West Point, that he was married.
