Since Dargue and Arnold were renewing their friendship in an atmosphere dominated by airplanes, At nold could not help feeling a certain pressure to return to flying. His retirement from the air, in the face of danger, had left a bitter sense of failure, which, for his own peace of mind, he ought to expunge. For more than four months he resisted the pressure to fly again, but eventually, whether or not he had to fly as a means of redeeming his self-respect was no longer the compelling question.
