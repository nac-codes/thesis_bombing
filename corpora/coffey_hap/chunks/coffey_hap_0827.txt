On his third day, after meeting high British air officers and American diplomats, he visited Beaverbrook, who proved to be one man in London with ideas as large as his own. Beaverbrook listened to Arnold talk about the planes and materiel and men he considered necessary to win the war, then called Churchill and suggested the prime minister interview this American airman.
