When Arnold addressed himself to King, the admiral was not above aiming his reply to Marshall, as if it were Marshall who had spoken, or as if it should be assumed that Marshall had put the words in Arnold’s mouth There was a tradition in military councils that equals addressed equals, but King, when he refused to address a full member of this body as an equal, was going far beyond tradition. He was telling an upstart he didn’t belong where he was. King was a battleship admiral who had only lately come to acknowledge the importance of air. Though he had earned his wings at the age of forty-nine, he was hardly an aviator. Sometimes he seemed to blame Arnold personally for the fact that airplanes were now threatening to make his battleships obsolete  Cooke was quite literally correct in his remarks to Anderson. Arnold was not a chief of staff. But neither was Leahy, although he was now called the president's chief of staff, whatever that might mean.
