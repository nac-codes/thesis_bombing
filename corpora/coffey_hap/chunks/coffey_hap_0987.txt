It was 3:30 a.m. British time (12:30 a.m. local time), June 3 before Arnold and his party landed at Goose Bay, after flying against head winds that cut their airspeed to 135 miles per hour. They rode in jeeps to the mess hall where they were served a greasy breakfast of roast beef, fried potatoes, and string beans, all cold.  Arnold said to the commanding officer, “What the hell kind of food is this for an American Air Forces base?”"*  The officer said, “We're sorry, General, but we can’t make much fuss about it because we have a contract with the Canadians and they run the mess.”  “I'll be damned if they're going to run a mess like this,” Arnold declared. “If our boys have to sit up here in this God-awful hole with no recreation, and in such a terrible climate, at least they're going to have  good food.”  Tam to one of his aides in  He took out his pen and wrote a tele; Washington.
