Chiang Kai-shek, using Claire Chennault as his spokesman, was demanding that the B-29s in China either be turned over to him or be moved to another theater. Though Arnold was ignoring this demand, he knew he had to get some action out of the Superforts against Japan soon, or Chiang, MacArthur, the Navy, and others who wanted them for local actions would have legitimate arguments for taking them from him.  LeMay was aware of the pressures upon Arnold, and he was also, even more keenly, aware of the pressures Arnold was already putting  upon him.
