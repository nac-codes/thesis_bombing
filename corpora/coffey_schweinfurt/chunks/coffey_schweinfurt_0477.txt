The Americans were on their way home when the first German fighters caught up to them. A German lance corporal named Erich Handke, who was in one of these planes—an ME-110—has recorded his reaction to his first sight of the big American bombers: ‘‘Suddenly we saw the Boeing Fortress IIs ahead in a great swarm. I confess the sight put me into a bit of a flap, and the others felt the same. We seemed so puny against these four-engined giants. Then we attacked from the beam.’’?!  Handke’s plane was one of an estimated sixty German fighters that met the Americans that day. They shot down one B-17 and two B-24s—not a prohibitive loss. American gunners claimed twenty- two victories. Even allowing for exaggeration (German figures claimed a loss of only seven fighters that day) the big bombers had made an auspicious debut against the Hitler homeland.
