On December 1, Blakeslee decided the new men were ready for a little action. He led them in their sleek and shiny Mustangs across the Channel to Calais and the Belgian coast, just far enough to give the Germans a good look at them. The German antiaircraft gunners got too good a look at some of them, perforating their bottoms with flak but doing no serious damage. On December 5 they accompanied the bombers for the first time in a shallow penetration of France, and on the 11th they made their first trip to Germany, escorting the bombers to Emden. A few German fighters appeared in the distance but did not engage them. Five days later, on the 16th, after their first combat, with ME- 109s and JU-88s, the Mustangs claimed their first victory.
