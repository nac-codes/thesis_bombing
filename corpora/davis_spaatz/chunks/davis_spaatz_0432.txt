By February 19, 1944, another part of Spaatz's command, the Fifteenth Air Force, would contribute an additional 12 heavy bomber and 4 fighter groups. 13 Before taking on the Luftwaffe, Spaatz instituted a system in which opera- tions and administration received equal attention. This arrangement stemmed from Spaatz's dual responsibilities as the Commander of USSTAF, in opera- tional control of the Eighth and Fifteenth Air Forces, and as the head of the AAF in Britain in charge of administration for the Eighth and Ninth Air Forces. The Ninth, a tactical air force, made up the U.S. contingent of Air Chief Marshal Leigh-Mallory's AEAF. Spaatz appointed Brig. Gen. Hugh J.
