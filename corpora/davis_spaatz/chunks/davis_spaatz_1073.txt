Roger Freeman's books on the Eighth Air Force, although written for trivia buffs, give an accurate and colorful picture of equipment, tactics, and operations of the American heavy-bomber forces in Europe. Weigley's book on north- western Europe is the best single account of the campaign, while D'Este has written an excellent account of Normandy. John Terraine's work on the RAF also is worthwhile. Frankland's short work for the Ballantine War Series presents an interesting contrast to his official endeavors. In the Ballantine work Frankland draws conclusions apparently denied to him in an official role; for instance, he gives Spaatz the laurel for winning the bomber offensive. Schaffer's book is a well-researched, balanced account of the moral dilemma faced by the AAF when the pursuit of victory led it beyond precision bombing to city bombing.
