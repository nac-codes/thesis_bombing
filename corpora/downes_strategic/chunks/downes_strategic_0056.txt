For this reason, U.S. political and military men expressed keen interest from the beginning in strategies that might provide a shortcut to victory, such as unrestricted submarine warfare and incendiary bombing. Although the United States was able to initiate submarine attacks against Japanese merchant shipping soon after Pearl Harbor, it could not bomb Japan until it acquired bases close to the country. The distance to be covered explains the two-and-a-half-year delay between the Japanese attack and the first sus-tained U.S. bombing of the home islands (in June 1944, launched from bases in China), but planning for a bombing campaign had long been underway and quickly focused on the potential of firebombing to defeat Japan.
