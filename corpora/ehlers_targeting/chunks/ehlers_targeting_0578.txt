Others followed shortly, taking jobs alongside their British hosts in A.I.3(c), which produced target materials, A.I.3(c)2, which produced executive-level bomb damage assessment, and A.I.3(U.S.A.), the Air Ministry office responsible for ensuring the efficient supply and distribution of all intelligence material and reports to USAAF headquarters and flying units. A.I.3(U.S.A.) conducted direct liaison with the CIU and ensured all required interpretation reports authored by K (Damage Assessment) Section, D (Industry) Section, and the other fifteen-plus sections reached American customers with minimum delay. To speed this process, the Air Ministry assigned a production code to every report. These were keyed to specific distribution lists, each with anywhere from 5 to 300 recipients. As soon as a product went to press, couriers were ready to speed them to all the organizations on each list.
