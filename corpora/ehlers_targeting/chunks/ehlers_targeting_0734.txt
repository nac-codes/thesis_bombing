With the start of Operation ARGUMENT on 24 January, the Americans set out to wrest control of the air over the Continent from their German adversaries. They won a resounding victory. This air campaign against the Luftwaffe and the German aircraft industry was ferocious. The United States Army Air Forces (USAAF) destroyed 3,700 German fighters and damaged another 1,400 at the cost of 946 bombers and 397 fighters. Although bombers did serious damage to aircraft, aeroengine, and ball-bearing plants, the decisive factor was the relentless destruction of Luftwaffe fighters and their increasingly inadequately trained pilots, who were compelled to engage the Americans in a losing effort to protect their sources of supply.
