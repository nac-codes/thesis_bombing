The Army Air Corps purchased twenty Sperry N-1s in 1938 for $320,000. Coupled with the A-2 automatic pilot, the N-1 became the O-1 in 1939. To keep the Sperry program alive, the Air Corps purchased 600 O-1s, but they were cumbersome and difficult to operate, and in 1941 the Air Corps turned them over to the British.
