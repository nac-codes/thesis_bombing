Chapter 2 The Casablanca Offensive: The Allies over Germany, 1943-44 At lunchtime on January 18, 1943, Air Vice Marshal John Slessor, RAF assistant chief of staff, sat on top of the roof of the Anfa Hotel in Casablanca watching “the long Atlantic rollers breaking on the beaches” while he sketched out a compromise agreement between the American and British chiefs of staff over the future of Allied strategy. Chief of Staff Charles Portal then read it through and changed a few words. In the list of strategic commitments jotted in his notebook, Slessor had included “The heaviest possible bomber offensive from the UK against GERMANY direct.”!
