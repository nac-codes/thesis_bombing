Against Chinese cities, area bombing apparently continued until for- bidden late in July 1945.1  The issue is not whether there were legitimate military targets in Formosa and China. There were and these were hit. Much more was hit. To some degree, the rationale was similar to the one used to justify the torching of Japan’s cities. As the official historians wrote, “So many of the significant targets of Formosa were situated in the island’s cities and towns that area bombing was frequently employed. The resulting destruction, it was assumed, not only would reach supplies of military importance and many small industrial units, but would impose upon the enemy, through destruction of housing and municipal services, a serious loss of labor.” But in this case the “municipal services” also supported friendly civilians, and the “serious loss of labor” was inflicted on the same population.
